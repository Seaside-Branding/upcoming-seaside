THE CONCEPT

Architecture shapes the way we live, interact with our environment, and understand the spaces around us. This conference introduces high school students to the world of architecture and design, offering them the opportunity to explore both the creative and practical dimensions of the profession. By bringing together architects, urban planners, designers, and structural engineers, the event creates a space for dialogue between students and specialists, helping young people gain a clearer understanding of what architecture involves.

Through a series of talks and discussions, participants are introduced to topics such as architectural styles, construction safety, environmentally responsible materials, and interior and spatial design. These conversations also explore the architectural design process, revealing how ideas evolve from initial concepts into functional and meaningful spaces.

Our Mission

Architecture is not typically included in the standard high school curriculum, which means that many students who are interested in the field have limited opportunities to explore it in depth. This conference creates a space where young people can discover architecture through direct interaction with professionals, gaining insights that go beyond theoretical knowledge.

Alongside the talks, interactive workshops allow participants to engage with the design process through practical activities and develop creativity, spatial awareness, and problem-solving skills while gaining a hands-on understanding of how architects approach functionality, aesthetics, and sustainability.

At the same time, the conference encourages curiosity, collaboration, and initiative among participants. By connecting students with professionals and exposing them to different perspectives within the field, the event helps them better understand the possibilities that architecture offers and supports them in exploring their interests and potential future paths.
